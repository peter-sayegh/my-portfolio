Electrical Engineering Projects (Graduate)

SignSpeak: Real-Time ASL Fingerspelling Recognition on a Microcontroller

An embedded AI system that performs real-time American Sign Language fingerspelling recognition entirely on-device, with no cloud dependency. Running on a Seeed Studio XIAO ESP32S3 Sense microcontroller, SignSpeak uses a 16,349-parameter depthwise-separable CNN (67.4 KB, INT8 quantized) achieving 97.4% accuracy on the Kaggle ASL benchmark, with end-to-end inference latency under 110 ms. The system outputs recognized letters to an OLED display with a 3-tier LED confidence indicator.

250 MS/s 8-bit Current-Steering DAC in 0.18 µm CMOS

Design of a high-speed digital-to-analog converter targeting wireline transmitter applications, delivering ≥1.5 Vpp,diff into a 100 Ω differential load with SFDR exceeding 66 dB at the TT corner. The architecture uses a 3+5 segmented current-steering topology with thermometer-encoded MSBs, per-cell 6-bit trim DACs for ±10% mismatch correction, and retiming latches reducing inter-switch skew to ~1–2 ps. DNL remains within ±2×10−4 LSB across all three process corners.

Fully Differential Switched-Capacitor Amplifier in 0.18 µm CMOS

Design of a fully differential switched-capacitor sample-and-hold amplifier with 2× gain, intended as the residue amplification stage in a 7-bit 16 MHz pipelined ADC. The core is a folded-cascode OTA achieving 56.64 dB DC gain, 71° phase margin, 33.1 dB gain margin, and 167 MHz closed-loop bandwidth, with a settling error of 0.09% and total power consumption of 486.72 µW. A switched-capacitor CMFB circuit stabilizes the output common-mode at 0.9 V, achieving 333.4 dB CMRR.

2-Stage Low-Noise Amplifier for Starlink Ka-Band (11.4–12.7 GHz)

Design of a two-stage GaAs FET LNA on Duroid 5880 substrate targeting the Starlink customer terminal downlink band. The NE3210S01 HEMT was selected for its 0.34 dB NFmin at 12 GHz. The first stage is noise-matched to minimize Friis cascade noise figure; the second stage is gain-matched using Smith chart techniques. The final design achieves S21 > 23 dB, NF < 0.9 dB, S11 < −12 dB, and S22 < −14 dB across the band, with unconditional stability (µ > 1) from 0.1–20 GHz, IIP3 = 1 dBm, and 100 mW DC power.

Runway Detection for Autonomous Aircraft using Digital Signal Processing Techniques

This report presents a digital signal processing (DSP) approach to runway detection for autonomous aircraft landing systems. The project leverages fundamental DSP techniques including edge detection, Hough transform, and frequency domain analysis to identify and track runway structures from aircraft camera imagery. The proposed system processes visual data through a multi-stage pipeline designed to extract geometric and textural features characteristic of runway environments.

All-Region DC Model for MOSFET with Physics-Based Parameter Extraction

A physics-based approach for extracting device parameters and developing an all-region DC model for MOS transistors with uniform substrate. A systematic parameter extraction methodology is employed, utilizing transconductance-based threshold voltage extraction, geometry correction via maximum slope extrapolation, and mobility degradation characterization. The model incorporates effective mobility dependence, body effect, and accounts for short-geometry effects.

Ultrafast Nonlinear Acoustics in Free-standing Membranes at the Nanoscale

This paper explores the properties of nonlinear acoustic strain wave propagation in solid crystals, integrating both experimental data analysis and numerical modeling. By introducing the Korteweg- De Vries (KdV) equation, we describe soliton propagation within certain nonlinear systems. The study simulates several physical scenarios, including the compression of a sequence of acoustic pulses, reflection at a free interface, and acoustic attenuation. These simulations provide insights into the complex behavior of nonlinear acoustic waves, which could have significant implications for the design and analysis of advanced materials and acoustic devices. This project was conducted at Laboratoire des Solides Irradiés (LSI) under the supervision of Dr. Vasily Temnov.

Rogue Waves Dynamics using Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs)

Rogue waves are rare, unpredictable surface waves that exceed twice the height of surrounding waves and can appear suddenly from unexpected directions. Unlike tsunamis, these localized phenomena—sometimes surpassing 30 meters—arise from nonlinear energy focusing and pose significant risks to even the largest ships.This study focuses on training PINNs to solve the self-focusing Non-Linear Schrödinger equation and model the balance between nonlinearity and viscosity. This internship took place at LadHyX under the supervision of Prof. Christophe Josserand in January-March 2025.

Predicting Travel Insurance Purchases Using Machine Learning

This project implemented several machine learning concepts to predict travel insurance purchases, focusing on key features like Annual Income and Travel History. Models like Logistic Regression, KNN, Neural Networks, and Decision Trees were trained on a dataset of 2,000 samples. The model helps insurance companies target potential buyers efficiently, improving marketing and resource allocation.
