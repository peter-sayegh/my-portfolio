Electrical Engineering Projects (Graduate)

Runway Detection for Autonomous Aircraft using Digital Signal Porcessing Techniques

This report presents a digital signal processing (DSP) approach to runway detection for autonomous aircraft landing systems. The project leverages fundamental DSP techniques including edge detection, Hough transform, and frequency domain analysis to identify and track runway structures from aircraft camera imagery. The proposed system processes visual data through a multi-stage pipeline designed to extract geometric and textural features characteristic of runway environments. This project demonstrates practical applications of classical signal processing theory to modern autonomous vehicle navigation challenges.

On-chip CMOS Feedback Amplifier Design

This report presents the design of a two-stage Miller-compensated operational transconductance amplifier in 0.25 μm CMOS achieving 95 dB open-loop gain, 81.5° phase margin, 407 kHz closed-loop bandwidth with 10 V/V gain, and 13.7 μV output noise while consuming 247 μA from ±1.25 V supplies.

All-Region DC Model for MOSFET with Physics-Based Parameter Extraction

This letter presents a physics-based approach for extracting device parameters and developing an all-region DC model for MOS transistors with uniform substrate. A systematic parameter extraction methodology is employed, utilizing transconductance-based threshold voltage extraction, geometry correction via maximum slope (g_m or transconductance) extrapolation, and mobility degradation characterization. The model incorporates effective mobility dependence, body effect, and accounts for short-geometry effects.

Ultrafast Nonlinear Acoustics in Free-standing Membranes at the Nanoscale

This paper explores the properties of nonlinear acoustic strain wave propagation in solid crystals, integrating both experimental data analysis and numerical modeling. By introducing the Korteweg- De Vries (KdV) equation, we describe soliton propagation within certain nonlinear systems. The study simulates several physical scenarios, including the compression of a sequence of acoustic pulses, reflection at a free interface, and acoustic attenuation. These simulations provide insights into the complex behavior of nonlinear acoustic waves, which could have significant implications for the design and analysis of advanced materials and acoustic devices. This project was conducted at Laboratoire des Solides Irradiés (LSI) under the supervision of Dr. Vasily Temnov.

Rogue Waves Dynamics using Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs)

Rogue waves are rare, unpredictable surface waves that exceed twice the height of surrounding waves and can appear suddenly from unexpected directions. Unlike tsunamis, these localized phenomena—sometimes surpassing 30 meters—arise from nonlinear energy focusing and pose significant risks to even the largest ships.This study focuses on training PINNs to solve the self-focusing Non-Linear Schrödinger equation and model the balance between nonlinearity and viscosity. This internship took place at LadHyX under the supervision of Prof. Christophe Josserand in January-March 2025.

Predicting Travel Insurance Purchases Using Machine Learning

This project implemented several machine learning concepts to predict travel insurance purchases, focusing on key features like Annual Income and Travel History. Models like Logistic Regression, KNN, Neural Networks, and Decision Trees were trained on a dataset of 2,000 samples. Despite class imbalance, Decision Trees and Gradient Boosting provided the best results with an accuracy of 83.4%. The model helps insurance companies target potential buyers efficiently, improving marketing and resource allocation.
